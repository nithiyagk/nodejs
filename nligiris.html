NILGIRIS

The Nilgiris, also known as the Blue Mountains, is a picturesque mountain range located in the southern
Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, tea plantations,
and diverse flora and fauna, the Nilgiris offer several famous places to visit. Here are some of them

Places you can visit in Nilgiris:

  • Ooty (Udhagamandalam)
  • Coonoor
  • Avalanche Lake
  • Kotagiri
  • Pykara Lake and Waterfalls
  • Glenmorgan
  • Mudumalai National Park
  • Needle Rock Viewpoint


    • Ooty (Udhagamandalam)

    Ooty is the most popular hill station in the Nilgiris. It is known for its pleasant climate,
    lush greenery, tea gardens, and colonial-era architecture. The Nilgiri Mountain Railway, Ooty Lake, Botanical Gardens,
    and Doddabetta Peak are some of the main attractions in Ooty.


    Coonoor

    Coonoor is another beautiful hill station in the Nilgiris, located about 20 kilometers from Ooty. It is known for its tea estates,
    scenic viewpoints, and trekking trails. Sim's Park, Lamb's Rock, Dolphin's Nose, and Highfield
    Tea Factory are popular tourist spots in Coonoor.


    Avalanche Lake

    Located about 28 kilometers from Ooty, Avalanche Lake is a serene and scenic lake surrounded by dense
    forests. It is a perfect spot for picnics, fishing, and nature walks. Permission is
    required from the forest department to visit the lake.


    Kotagiri

    Kotagiri is a quiet hill station nestled in the Nilgiris and is known for its tea and coffee
    plantations. The Kodanad View Point, Catherine Falls, Rangaswamy Pillar, and Longwood Shola are
    some of the attractions in and around Kotagiri.


    Pykara Lake and Waterfalls

    Situated about 21 kilometers from Ooty, Pykara is known for its pristine lake and enchanting
    waterfalls. The Pykara Lake offers boating facilities, while the Pykara
    Waterfalls are a sight to behold during the monsoon season.


    Glenmorgan

    Located about 25 kilometers from Ooty, Glenmorgan is known for its beautiful tea gardens and
    the Glenmorgan Trek, which offers
    stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.


    Mudumalai National Park

    Situated on the northwestern side of the Nilgiris, Mudumalai National Park is a well-known wildlife sanctuary.
    It is home to various animal species, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and numerous bird species.
    Safari tours are available for visitors to explore the park.


    Needle Rock Viewpoint

    Located near Gudalur, Needle Rock Viewpoint offers panoramic views of
    the surrounding valleys and the picturesque landscape of the Nilgiris.
